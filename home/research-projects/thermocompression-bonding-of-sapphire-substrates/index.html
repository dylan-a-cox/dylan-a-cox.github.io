Thermocompression Bonding of Sapphire Substrates

Building sapphire sensors requires being able to stick sapphires together, and nothing sticks quite like a direct bond!  Nothing particularly groundbreaking here, but the pictures look pretty!

Bonding is done in a spark plasma sintering machine, under vacuum.  This machine runs pressures in the multi-MPa range and temperatures >1200C.  Current work involves investigating the effects of plasma-activation of the substrate surface, and getting delicate microphone diaphragms to survive without fracturing under the intense conditions.
