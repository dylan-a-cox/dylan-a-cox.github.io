Optoelectronics Development

As part of a research project into optical pressure and shear stress sensors, I developed and characterized a high sensitivity custom optoelectronics box for interfacing with fiber-based MEMS sensors.  With modern op-amps and photodiodes, it isn't all that difficult to create a shot-noise limited optoelectronics setup when illumination is good and bandwidth requirements are modest.  However, being able to accurately model and characterize the performance of such a setup is still a useful skill.

Figure 1: Photo of the completed optoelectronics setup

Both the frequency response and noise floor were analytically modeled and characterized for this optoelectronics setup with high relative accuracy. 

Frequency Response Characterization:

To characterize the frequency response, a stepped sine was fed from a function generator to modulate an LED.  This output was split between the optoelectronics under test and a Newfocus photoreceiver as a reference channel.  The outputs were recorded on a data acquisition unit and spectral estimation techniques were used to estimate the frequency response function.

Figure 2: Diagram of the experimental setup for characterizing frequency response
Figure 3: Frequency response results for each channel

Noise Floor Characterization:

To simulate the output of the sensor, a fiber-coupled LED was driven with a resistor bank.  A battery-operated preamplifier was used to provide AC-coupling and boost the optoelectronics output to utilize the full dynamic range of the data acquisition system.  Noise floor data was taken for 5 minutes at each channel, showing that the optical shot noise is almost two orders of magnitude higher than the intrinsic noise of the optoelectronics setup under expected operating conditions.
